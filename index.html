OUR STORY

We started because
silence was killing women.

Teal Trails began as a student initiative at Oakridge International School. We saw the numbers. We saw the stigma. We decided we would not look away.
Vaibhavi Babu
Founder • Oakridge Gachibowli, DP2

"I created Teal Trails because I couldn't accept that thousands of women in our country die every year from something we can prevent. This isn't just a health issue — it's an issue of access, education, and dignity."

What started as one student's frustration has grown into a movement of students, doctors, and communities working together to make cervical cancer a disease of the past in India.

THE SCIENCE

It develops slowly.
It can be stopped.

STAGE 1
HPV Infection

Most people get it. Most clear it naturally.

STAGE 2
Persistent Infection

High-risk strains (16 & 18) begin damaging cells over years.

STAGE 3
Pre-cancer (CIN)

Detectable and completely treatable at this stage.

STAGE 4
Invasive Cancer

This is where survival rates drop dramatically. This is what we are trying to prevent.

THE TRUTH

Nearly 99.7% of cervical cancers are caused by persistent high-risk HPV infection. This is a slow, detectable, and interruptible process. Every death represents a failure of awareness and access — not inevitability.

THREE LAYERS OF DEFENSE
1
HPV Vaccination (Primary Prevention)

Best given between ages 9–14. India's own Cervavac makes this accessible.

2
Screening (Secondary Prevention)

HPV DNA test every 5 years or Pap smear. Early detection saves lives.

3
Early Treatment

Pre-cancerous lesions can be treated with simple outpatient procedures.
