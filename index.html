Who We Are

Born from real conversations across India.

Teal Trails began as a cervical cancer and women's health initiative, rooted in conversations with girls in orphanages, women in rural mandals, and college students in Hyderabad who had never once heard the word "cervix." Over 77,000 women in India lose their lives to cervical cancer every year, not because medicine failed them, but because silence did.

The camps taught us something we didn't expect, though. As we went door to door, we kept encountering need that went far beyond what we'd come for, children with infections no one had treated, families without clean water, and much more. We couldn't bring ourselves to look away. So Teal Trails grew alongside us. What started as a cervical cancer initiative has become something larger — a community health movement still close to its roots in women's health, but reaching toward everyone we meet along the way.

We're here to break that silence, in classrooms, communities, and at doorsteps, because every person deserves to be heard, informed, and cared for.

How We Grow

We run awareness sessions in schools, colleges, and clinics — reaching over 100 adolescents before HPV exposure peaks. Our sessions cover HPV transmission, the link to cervical cancer, vaccine safety, and what screening actually involves.

We don't just deliver information and leave. We partner with local NGOs and Asha workers so the impact continues long after we've gone. Every conversation is designed to stay with the person long after the session ends.

What We Protect

Teal Trails is pioneering mobile health units in youth-led public health. Our teams bring certified medical professionals directly to doorsteps in villages where reliable healthcare is still out of reach.

Each mobile unit provides cervical cancer awareness along with anaemia screening, antenatal care for pregnant women, diabetes and hypertension management, and treatment for premature menopause. We eliminate the need for long waits in crowded hospitals and instead deliver comprehensive care where the system hasn't reached yet.

Camp Pragati

Progress, taken to the doorstep.

In early 2025, Teal Trails drove a CSR-funded mobile health unit into Thunki Kalsa, a village in rural Hyderabad. Here, chronic illnesses often go undiagnosed, reproductive health remains unspoken, and quality medical care is far from most families.

Our team addressed hypertension, diabetes, anaemia, premature menopause, and antenatal care — all while providing cervical cancer awareness. Every woman who received a consultation also walked away with educational materials and Teal Trails pamphlets. High school students attended a doctor-led seminar, many hearing about cervical cancer prevention for the very first time.

The Reality

Why It Matters

We met girls who had no language for their own reproductive health — not because they weren't curious, but because every adult around them had been taught the same silence. This isn't only a rural problem. Across privileged households and urban schools alike, stigma delays screening and turns a preventable disease into a death sentence.

We replace that shame with conversations that save lives. Because when a young girl understands her body, she gains the power to protect it.
