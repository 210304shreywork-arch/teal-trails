WHO WE ARE

Students who believe change begins with one conversation.

Teal Trails is a student-run initiative dedicated to improving access, awareness, and understanding of women's healthcare in India — with a strong focus on cervical cancer prevention and breaking stigma.

We believe healthcare should never be a challenge to pursue. It should reach every woman, regardless of where she lives or what she earns.

IN THE FIELD

Our Story

Teal Trails began as a student initiative at Oakridge International School. We saw the numbers. We saw the stigma. We decided we would not look away.

What started as one student's frustration has grown into a movement of students, doctors, and communities working together to make cervical cancer a disease of the past in India.

Vaibhavi Babu
Founder • Oakridge Gachibowli, DP2

"I created Teal Trails because I couldn't accept that thousands of women in our country die every year from something we can prevent."

The Complete Awareness Guide

How Cervical Cancer Develops

Nearly 99.7% of cervical cancers are caused by persistent high-risk HPV infection. This is a slow, detectable, and interruptible process. Every death represents a failure of awareness and access — not inevitability.

STAGE 1
HPV Infection

Most infections clear naturally. Persistent high-risk types begin damaging cells over years.

STAGE 2
Pre-cancer (CIN)

Detectable through screening. Fully treatable with cryotherapy or LEEP.

THREE LAYERS OF DEFENSE
1
HPV Vaccination

Best given between ages 9–14. India's Cervavac makes this accessible.

2
Screening

HPV DNA test every 5 years or Pap smear. Early detection saves lives.

3
Early Treatment

Pre-cancerous lesions can be treated with simple outpatient procedures.
